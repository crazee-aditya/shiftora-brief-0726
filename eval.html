Part B · the backtest protocol, published

The accuracy figures in the main document are produced by this protocol. Client data itself is not published; the method is, in full, so it can be challenged line by line.

  • Holdout, frozen at kickoff. The most recent 12 months of history are sealed before any model work begins. No model, feature, or rule ever trains on the holdout.
  • Rolling-origin evaluation. The model forecasts each holdout month from an origin that only sees data before it, then the origin rolls forward one month and repeats. Twelve origins, twelve out-of-sample forecasts.
  • Metrics. Accuracy is reported as 100 minus WAPE, where WAPE is the sum of absolute errors over the sum of actuals across the holdout. Bias, the signed error, is reported beside it and gated at plus or minus 3 percent so accuracy cannot be bought with systematic over- or under-forecasting.
  • Baseline first. The incumbent method, category averages or the existing planning sheet, is scored under the identical protocol before ours is. The improvement claim is the difference between the two, same data, same window, same math.
  • Re-proven every cycle. The backtest re-runs on every retrain and every rule change. A figure that stops passing stops being claimed.
